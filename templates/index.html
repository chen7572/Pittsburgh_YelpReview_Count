Data and Analysis

Yelp Data Challenge Dataset and Zillow Neighborhood Boundaries are used. Yelp dataset includes business information and review data for each business from 2005 to November 2017, which are JSON files. Pittsburgh neighborhood boundaries are extracted from Pennsylvania neighborhood shapefile. The two sources of data are joined together and formatted using Pandas, with Year-Month as index and review counts for each neighborhood as values.

Yelp launched iPhone app in 2008 and Android app in late 2009. To take into account of reviews from both desktop and mobile users, review data after 2010 was used in time series analysis. For several neighborhoods, such as the Central Business District and Southside Flats, review counts increased exponentially, so the data was log-transformed before fitting a time series model.

Both decomposing the review counts for Central Business District and Fourier transforming the data to frequency domain revealed a seasonal component of 12-month. As a result, the SARIMAX (Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average with eXogenous regressors) model in Python StatsModel was selected to fit the data. Data between January 2010 and December 2016 was used as training dataset to find the model parameters through gridsearch for each neighborhood. The parameters being searched include the order of difference [0, 1], the AR term [0, 1, 2], the MA term [0, 1, 2] and the corresponding terms for the seasonal component. The model performance was evaluated on the test data from January 2017 to November 2017. After choosing the model parameters, data between 2010 and 2017 were used to fit the model, and prediction on the review count was made for December 2017 to December 2018.

About Me

My interest in data analysis and finding insight from analysis results has driven me to explore the field of Data Science.
